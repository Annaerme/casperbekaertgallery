Gallery in the Distillery

Art met craft. Contemporary works sparked curiosity and celebrated the space — transforming the Filliers Distillery into a site of imagination and discovery.

Ten artists responded to the industrial grandeur of the distillery with works ranging from painting to installation — each bringing a distinct voice to a space defined by copper kettles, oak barrels, and the scent of grain.

Over two weekends, 1.200 visitors discovered the intersection of contemporary art and craft heritage beyond the traditional white walls.

Group exhibition Filliers Distillery, Ertvelde 6–8 & 14–15 March 2026 1.200 visitors

Participating artists

Alex Brassart

Works with fragments sourced from films, documentaries, found images, and television broadcasts. His paintings carry a subtle, restless atmosphere and open up spaces for interpretation.

Arno Camps

Antwerp-based multidisciplinary artist whose work constructs a 'counter-reality' questioning authority, social control, and imposed norms.

Jan De Lauré

Creates modest, grand work shaped by persistent memories. Works from images that have lodged themselves in his perception and refuse to be forgotten.

Jan Vindevogel

Belgian painter engaging with classical traditions, unfolding meaning through time and slow, attentive viewing.

Jeroen Frateur

Reflects on unfamiliar spaces as strange, almost magical worlds where not understanding becomes an invitation to look, remember, and imagine.

Manon Soetens

Paints intimate figures from memory within quiet landscapes. Her work invites slow looking — not to explain, but to feel.

Martha Onderwater

Explores the human body through sculpture, painting, and drawing. Blends abstraction with realism, emphasising strength and elegance.

Randoald Sabbe

Explores language, history, and identity through shifting layers of meaning. Balancing between visibility and concealment.

Stief De Smet

Multidisciplinary practice exploring the ambiguous relationship between nature and culture. Works with subtle irony and conceptual precision.

William Ludwig Lutgens

Belgian painter blending dreamlike imagery exploring ego and media influence. International exhibitions spanning Europe and beyond since 2017.
