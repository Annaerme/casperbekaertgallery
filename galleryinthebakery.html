Gallery in the Bakery

Art and a unique space came together in an accessible, one-of-a-kind experience. The bakery's architecture — cold storage, ovens, flour-dusted walls — became the conceptual foundation of the exhibition.

Seven artists responded to the space with works ranging from painting to sculpture. Where the rhythm of labour once produced the smell of fresh bread, a different kind of alchemy took place: a merging of images, materials, and ideas.

Not a reconstruction, not a conventional gallery — a search for balance between form and content, between intuition and concept. Over three days, 750 visitors discovered art in a setting that transformed the familiar into something unforgettable.

Group exhibition Bakkerij Bekaert, Ertvelde 10–12 October 2025 750 visitors

Discover the Artists

Alex Brassart

Works with images from films, documentaries, and found materials, exploring the intangible and the underlying in his paintings. His work invites an ongoing dialogue between image, past, and viewer.

Cas Reynders

Rooted in authentic craftsmanship and the use of local materials. His designs are pure, timeless creations that bridge tradition and contemporary design, with a deep appreciation for cultural heritage.

Edo Maas

Turned from cycling to art in 2021. From his Antwerp studio, he creates sculptures that capture raw human emotion in a personal yet universal language.

Jean De Groote

Often called the "painter of silence." He isolates simple, everyday objects from their context and distills them to their essence, evoking a meditative atmosphere in his still lifes.

Orlando Gruwez

Explores the boundaries between language, image, and concept. His work is a continuous search for new forms of expression that connect thought and feeling.

Randoald Sabbe

Intertwines history, language, and symbolism in his art, exploring the ambiguity and layered nature of meaning. His works invite reflection and a deeper dialogue between past and present.

Emile Desweemer

Creates collage-like paintings that merge abstraction and figuration into evocative memories, playfully visualising heavy themes such as mourning, pain and acceptance, while enveloping the viewer in a melancholic and ominous atmosphere.
